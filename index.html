Hello!

I'm Daniel Elkik, a Computer Science student in the Daedalus Scholars Program at Hunter College. I became attached to the field after realizing the versitality that accompanies coding. Anything is possible as long as I have a computer and my fingers to type. I'm interested in both Finance and Game Development. My motivation in life is to create projects through the medium of video games in order to inspire, and of course entertain, people of all ages and countries, especially when life becomes tough. Recently, the FDA approved a video game as a prescription for ADHD. This is an incredible achievement, one that could finally end the negative stigma that surrounds video games and one that I would love to be a part of one day. So until then, I will continue coding and creating projects so I can reach this dream of mine.

One of my favorite hobbies includes drawing/sketching. I really love spending some of my time drawing with either a pen and paper or a tablet. I prefer traditional, however, as the pencil moving across the computer paper is incredibly relaxing. My best art comes from replicating a picture I see and recently I have gotten fond of drawing ducks. Another hobby is coding small games in pygame and soon, in Unity using C#. A few games that have been inspirations for me that I recommend are the Yakuza Series and Dark Souls. Yakuza has a dramatic undertone in its story while also presenting "minigames" to have fun with when you aren't progressing in the story, such as karaoke or bowling. Dark Souls is well known for balancing its challenging difficulty and engaging gameplay. Both games serve as great references for what makes a video game fun.

My Projects!

Personal and school projects (with more to come!)

Space Force

Space Force is a two-week group project that was presented at the Hunter College Codefest at Google. This Space Invader's themed project is an educational children's game, made in Python, meant to teach them one fun fact about each of the planets while also presenting a challenge. They only have three lives and shooting the wrong planet will cause a Game Over. Within the team behind the project, I had worked on the Object-Oriented Programing with one other member and a few design choices while the rest of the work had been split up with the other three members.
